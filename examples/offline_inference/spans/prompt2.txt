In the kingdom of Luminthia, where crystalline towers spiraled toward azure skies and gardens bloomed with flowers that sang at twilight, there existed a peculiar workshop at the edge of the royal grounds. This was the domain of Master Artificer Cornelius, a man whose genius had given the kingdom automated fountains, self-harvesting orchards, and mechanisms that had revolutionized daily life. But his greatest creation—though he did not yet know it—was Unit Seven-Theta-Nine, a maintenance automaton designed to tend the palace's vast network of mechanical systems. The robot, as everyone called it, stood precisely six feet tall, constructed of burnished brass and steel with joints of intricate gearing. Its chest cavity housed a core of perpetual motion, a marvel of engineering that gave it seemingly endless energy. Its optical sensors glowed a soft amber, and its hands, though capable of tremendous precision, possessed a strength that could bend iron bars. Seven-Theta-Nine had been designed for utility, not beauty, yet there was something oddly graceful in the economy of its movements, the quiet efficiency with which it performed its duties. For three years, the robot had fulfilled its programming without incident. It repaired clockwork, maintained the heating systems, kept the elaborate astronomical clock in the Grand Hall perfectly synchronized with the heavens. It knew every corridor, every hidden passage, every mechanical secret of the palace. But it knew nothing of the human heart, for it possessed none.
